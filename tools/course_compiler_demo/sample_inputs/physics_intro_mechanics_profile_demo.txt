# AxiomIQ-Owned Intro Physics Mechanics Source: Vectors to Newton's Second Law

Course-level signal: INTRO_PHYSICS_MECHANICS. Subject signal: PHYSICS.

This AxiomIQ-authored source introduces a short mechanics pathway:
Vectors -> Net Force -> Newton's Second Law.

## Topic: Vectors

Learning objectives:
- Students will resolve a force into x and y components.
- Students will connect force components to later net-force calculations.

Practice problem 1:
A 10 N force is directed 30 degrees above the horizontal. Resolve the force components in the x and y directions.

## Topic: Net Force

Learning objectives:
- Students will sum forces along a line to compute net force.
- Students will treat rightward force as positive unless a problem states otherwise.

Explicit prerequisite relationship:
Before computing net force in one dimension, students should be able to resolve a force into components when a force is angled.

Practice problem 2:
A cart has a 9 N push to the right and a 4 N friction force to the left. Find the one-dimensional resultant force.

## Topic: Newton's Second Law

Learning objectives:
- Students will apply Newton's Second Law to connect net force, mass, and acceleration.
- Students will use the formula F_net = m a.

Explicit prerequisite relationship:
Applying Newton's Second Law depends on the ability to compute net force first.

Practice problem 3:
A 4 kg cart experiences a net horizontal force of 12 N. Use F_net = m a to determine its acceleration.

Practice problem 4:
A 2 kg block accelerates at 1.5 m/s^2. Use F_net = m a to determine the net force.

Assessment potential:
The source supports a short internal check with numeric answers and units.

Practice potential:
The source supports original practice-style questions for vectors, net force, and Newton's second law.
